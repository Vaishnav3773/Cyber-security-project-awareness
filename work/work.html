Workshops and Activities

We organized interactive workshops and awareness drives in schools, colleges, and local communities. Participants were taught how to identify phishing links, set strong passwords, and handle suspicious messages safely.

Objectives

  • To increase awareness about online security threats.
  • To promote safe practices for social media and online transactions.
  • To educate rural and urban communities about cyber hygiene.
  • To reduce vulnerability to cyber scams and data theft.
  • To create a lasting impact through community-based learning.

Scammers often attract them through fake links, free gift offers, online games, scholarship forms, or job messages that look genuine. When students click on these links or share personal details, scammers steal their information or install harmful software on their devices.

Vaishnav discussing real cybercrime cases

According to multiple reports from The Times of India, Hindustan Times, and Indian Express, hundreds of students across India have lost money or personal data due to fake online schemes. Sharing real cases through newspapers, posters, or school notice boards can help students recognize such threats.

Analyzing recent cyber fraud cases

In recent years, several newspapers and online news portals have reported cases where students have become victims of online scams. Reading newspaper articles about online fraud keeps people updated about the latest scams and teaches them to stay alert. Schools can also organize newspaper reading sessions or discussions on cybercrime news to spread awareness more effectively.

Demonstrating safe online practices

Nowadays, many people become victims of online scams because of a lack of awareness. Scammers use fake messages, calls, and websites to steal personal information and money. Students must be careful while sharing details like OTPs, passwords, or bank information online.

Community cybersecurity awareness session

Students often get trapped by fake calls, messages, or offers pretending to be from banks or scholarships. Scammers ask for OTP, PIN, or account details to steal money. Precautions: Never share personal banking info, verify messages with your bank, and report suspicious activity immediately.

Community cybersecurity awareness session

Students gathered for a special school assembly to learn about the dangers of online scams and the importance of cyber safety. The session helped create awareness on how to identify fraud, protect personal information, and stay safe while using the internet. Through this program, students were encouraged to be alert, think smart, and report any suspicious online activity..

Conclusion

The Cyber Security Awareness Project successfully empowered individuals to protect themselves from online threats. By fostering awareness and practical skills, it contributed to creating a more secure and informed digital community. Future plans include expanding the program to more schools and community centers.
